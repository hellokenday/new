Design system

HeyBryan

Introduction

HeyBryan is a startup that connects home service experts with anyone who needs tasks done around the home.

Having two mobile apps in the pipeline, and a rapidly growing team, it became essential to unite departments and products around a common visual language.

My role

Create and document the design system involving:

  • Defining design principles
  • Auditing interface patterns
  • Establishing a pattern library
  • Creating a master design file
  • Documenting patterns and guidelines on how to use them

Problem

After conducting stakeholder interviews and expert reviews, it was clear that the platform was inconsistent and resembled a patchwork experience.

Problem areas

Reviewing the platform and carrying out a quick interface inventory revealed the following issues.

Inefficient patterns

Multiple design patterns solving the same problem

Reduced velocity

Increasing design and technical debt slowing down product output

Inconsistencies

Discrepant styles and interface designs weakening the brand

Solution

The main solution was to create a design system and document our new visual language.

A design system is a collection of reusable components, guided by clear standards, that can be assembled together to build any number of applications.

Goals

The main goals for this project were to optimize the experience and speed up our sprints.

Increase design and engineering velocity
Unify the brand and overall experience across all platforms
Enable the product to scale while reducing design and tech debt

Step 1: Define design principles

Design principles are shared guidelines that capture the essence of what good design means for a team, and advice on how to achieve it.

We started by defining our design principles to ensure the purpose of the product manifested in the design and to help articulate the personality we want users to experience.

Purpose and ethos

The purpose is the core of the product and should inform design and development decisions. Based on our understanding of who our users are, their goals, needs and motivations, we expressed the purpose as:

Connect caring experts with house proud customers and enrich them with a personable and trustworthy experience.

Pricing clarity

Question

How might we explain that there is an annual membership fee and sitters pet sit for free in exchange for accommodation?

Solution

Showing information cards about our pricing model which also direct users to our how it works page. Include information about pricing on our how it works page.

Hypothesis

We believe that showing information cards about our pricing model which also direct users to our how it works page for first-time visitors will explain there is an annual membership fee and sitters pet sit for free in exchange for accommodation.

Testing hypothesis

We ran usability tests to show information cards about our pricing model which also directs users to our how it works page. We then asked follow-up questions to identify the user's understanding of the pricing model. The majority of participants understood that there is an annual membership fee and sitters petsit for free.

Impact

  • 2% increase in partial sitter conversion
  • 1% increase in partial owner conversion
  • Redesign
  • Old design
  • User journey solution

Increase trust

Question

How might we ensure people feel they can trust our service and be comfortable letting strangers into their homes?

Solution

Directing users to information about our safety verifications and code of conduct

Hypothesis

We believe that directing users to information about our safety verifications and code of conduct for first-time visitors will ensure people feel they can trust our service and be comfortable letting strangers into their homes.

Testing hypothesis

We ran usability tests to direct users to information about our safety verifications and code of conduct. We then asked follow-up questions to identify the user's emotion associated with our service (trustworthy, safe, legit). The majority of participants identified our service as safe or trustworthy and they would be willing to become a member.

Impact

  • 3% increase in partial sitter conversion
  • 4% increase in partial owner conversion
  • Redesign
  • Old design
  • User journey solution

Outcome

The solution areas helped drive acquistion and created a good foundation for future experiments. Feedback from customer service has been positive, with fewer questions asked about pricing, trust, and how it works.
